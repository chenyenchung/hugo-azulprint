People keep asking what changed. The answer is that very little did.

The work I did in the forties was the work I was doing in the seventies, and it
is the work I am doing now. What changed is that techniques caught up — others
could finally see in molecules what we had been counting in kernels — and the
field decided, on its own schedule, that the controlling elements were not an
exception after all.

## On method

If I have a method, it is this. Spend long enough with one organism that you
can tell when something is unusual without first having to define what
"unusual" means. The maize plant has been very patient with me. I have tried to
return the favor by not assuming, in advance, what it ought to be doing.

The genome rearranges itself. It always did. We were simply not in the habit of
looking.
