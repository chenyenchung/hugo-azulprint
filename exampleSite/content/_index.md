title: "Barbara McClintock"
---

Currently I work with maize and the controlling elements that move through its
genome — how a chromosome can break, rejoin, and silence a locus in one
generation, then release it in the next.

I came to this from cytology. A decade spent staining pachytene nuclei in *Zea
mays* taught me to read chromosomes as objects with structure and history, not
as static catalogues of genes. The variegated kernel is what kept me here: a
phenotype you can count by eye, set against a genome that turned out to be far
more mobile than anyone expected. I have a longer-running interest in what the
genome does under stress — the kinds of reorganizations that look like noise
until you watch them for long enough to see the pattern.
